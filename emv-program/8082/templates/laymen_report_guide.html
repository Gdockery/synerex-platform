üìö Glossary - Understanding the Technical Terms

kWh (Kilowatt-Hour)
The unit of measurement for electricity usage. Like gallons for water or miles for distance. Your electric bill shows how many kWh you used.
THD (Total Harmonic Distortion)
A measure of electrical "noise" in your system. Lower is better - it means cleaner power for your equipment.
Power Factor
A measure of how efficiently your electrical system uses power. Higher is better - it means less waste.
IEEE 519
The industry standard for power quality. Meeting this standard means your electrical system is operating at professional levels.
ASHRAE Guideline 14
The industry standard for measuring energy savings accurately. It ensures our measurements are reliable and professional.
Statistical Significance
A measure of how confident we are that the results are real and not just random variation. 95% confidence means we're very sure.
Confidence Interval
The range of likely savings. For example, "between $500 and $700 per month" gives you a realistic expectation.
Payback Period
How long it takes for your investment to pay for itself through energy savings. A 2-year payback means you'll break even in 2 years.
